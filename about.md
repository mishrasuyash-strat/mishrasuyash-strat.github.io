I work at the intersection of applied AI product leadership and independent
research. In industry, I lead the design of AI systems for high-stakes,
regulated settings — the kind of environment where a decision has to be not just
good, but *explainable* and *defensible* after the fact. On my own time, I
formalise the ideas that make that possible and publish them openly.

## What I work on

- **Multi-agent systems** — coordination, arbitration, and getting many
  specialised agents to produce one coherent, trustworthy answer.
- **Causal inference & decision-making** — moving from correlation to
  interventions you can actually stand behind under uncertainty.
- **Game theory & equilibria** — modelling strategic behaviour and personalised
  decisions as equilibria rather than one-shot predictions.
- **Foundations** — category theory, sheaf theory, and topology as tools for
  gluing local judgments into a global, verifiable whole.

## Selected directions

My recent papers span verifier-centered agentic orchestration, equilibrium-guided
personalization, evolutionary memory for open-ended discovery, and the more formal
end of things — topos-theoretic reasoning and sharp regularity theory. The full
list, with links, is on the [papers]({% link papers.html %}) page.
