I work at the intersection of applied AI product leadership and independent
research. In industry I lead the design and delivery of AI systems for
high-stakes, regulated environments — the kind of setting where a decision has
to be not just good, but *explainable* and *defensible* after the fact. On my
own time I formalise the ideas that make that possible and publish them openly.

## In industry

I'm a senior AI product leader at Roche in Zürich, where I am part of enterprise
generative-AI programme and lead a cross-functional team to drive innovation and 
scalibility of AI solution. The flagship is **Intelligent Content Hub** — a
platform that takes modular, compliant healthcare content from analysis through
build and circulation end to end. In production it has roughly halved content
production time and cut cost by more than 60%, alongside markedly faster
medical-legal review and a large jump in search relevance over the system it
replaced.

The work spans the full stack of applied AI: LLM and retrieval architectures,
multi-agent workflows, vision-language systems for long-form video, and the
evaluation, observability, and governance layers that make all of it safe to
ship under MLR/GxP constraints. Across roles I've spent 14+ years building
production AI in regulated industries — pharmaceutical, energy, and automotive.

## Research

Independently, I publish on the theory underneath that practice — multi-agent
coordination, causal inference, game theory, and memory for long-horizon agents.
Recent preprints, all on the [papers]({% link papers.html %}) page, cover
verifier-centered agentic orchestration, equilibrium-guided personalization, and
evolutionary memory for open-ended discovery, along with more formal work on
topos-theoretic reasoning. Two frameworks from this research — a causal decision
unit for scoring interventions, and an equilibrium-guided approach to
personalization — have carried back into how I design products.

Earlier work sits in natural-language processing and computational biology, with
publications in venues including *BMC Bioinformatics* and *Cell Reports Medicine*.

## What I work on

- **Multi-agent systems** — coordination, arbitration, and getting many
  specialised agents to produce one coherent, trustworthy answer.
- **Causal inference & decision-making** — moving from correlation to
  interventions you can actually stand behind under uncertainty.
- **Game theory & equilibria** — modelling strategic behaviour and personalised
  decisions as equilibria rather than one-shot predictions.
- **Foundations** — category theory, sheaf theory, and topology as tools for
  gluing local judgments into a global, verifiable whole.
